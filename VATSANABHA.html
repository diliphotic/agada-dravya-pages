Introduction

Aconitum ferox Wall. ex Ser., commonly known as Vatsanābha, is a highly poisonous medicinal plant belonging to the family Ranunculaceae. In Ayurveda, it is classified under उपविष.

Due to its intense toxicity in crude form, the plant is a perennial herb predominantly found in the Himalayan regions of India, growing at high altitudes. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Traditional / Ayurvedic Perspective

Described in Ayurveda as स्थावर विष and considered महाविष in unpurified form. After proper शोधन, it becomes अमृत and acts as दीपनीय and पाचनीय.

After Śodhana, it possesses ज्वरघ्न and शूलहर properties and is used in ज्वर, आमवात, वात व्याधि, सन्धिवात, शूल.

Unpurified drug aggravates वात and पित्त दोष.

Modern Perspective

Contains toxic diterpene alkaloids, mainly aconitine. Acts as a potent neurotoxin and cardiotoxin.

Alters voltage-gated sodium channels in nerves and heart causing persistent depolarization. Leads to paresthesia, numbness, muscle weakness, cardiac conduction disturbances and fatal arrhythmias.

Fatal Dose & Fatal Period

2–4 mg of aconitine alkaloids
OR approximately 1–2 g of cured root
