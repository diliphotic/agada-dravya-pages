ताम्रकूट (TĀMRAKŪṬA / TOBACCO)
Upaviṣa | Neurotoxic Plant Poison

Introduction

Tāmrakūṭa, commonly known as Tobacco, is a well-known plant of toxicological importance in both Ayurvedic and modern medicine. It is widely used as an addictive substance and is recognized for its potent alkaloid nicotine, which exerts significant effects on the nervous system. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

Morphology / Identification

Tobacco is a tall, erect, annual herb with large, ovate to lanceolate leaves. Leaves are sticky, green, and contain abundant glandular hairs. Flowers are tubular, pinkish-red, arranged in terminal inflorescences.

Habitat / Distribution

Tobacco is cultivated worldwide, especially in tropical and subtropical regions. It is extensively grown in India, China, Brazil, and the USA.

Active Principles / Constituents

The leaves contain toxic alkaloids such as Nicotine, Lobeline, Anabasine, and Nornicotine. Nicotine content in dried leaves ranges from 1–8%.

Mode of Action

Nicotine → Stimulation of autonomic ganglia → Initial sympathetic & parasympathetic stimulation → CNS stimulation → Subsequent CNS depression → Ganglionic blockade and neuromuscular paralysis

Signs & Symptoms

Acute Toxicity:
Excitement, vomiting, tachycardia, bradycardia, hypotension, convulsions, dizziness, irritability, collapse.

Chronic Toxicity:
Dyspnea, cough, bronchitis, anemia, sore throat, anorexia, lung cancer.

Traditional / Ayurvedic Perspective

रस: तिक्त, कटु

Tāmrakūṭa is considered sharp, hot, kapha-vāta destroying but aggravates rakta and pitta and is described as poison-like in action.

Modern Medical Perspective

Nicotine acts as a stimulant at low doses and a depressant at higher doses. It affects both central and peripheral nervous systems, leading to addiction and systemic toxicity.

Fatal Dose & Fatal Period

Fatal Dose:
Nicotine – 60–100 mg
Tobacco – 15–30 g

Fatal Period: 5–15 minutes

Detoxification / Śodhana

Physical Methods: Washing, soaking, heat treatment
Chemical Methods: Neutralization with acids/alkalis, oxidation
Biological Methods: Microbial and enzymatic degradation
Ayurvedic Śodhana: Virechana, Nasya, Śamana and Rasāyana therapy

Therapeutic Uses

Traditionally used externally for pain relief and parasitic infestations, though internal use is discouraged due to toxicity.

Formulations and Disease Indication

Used in limited external formulations under strict supervision in Ayurvedic practice.

Post-mortem Appearance

Blue discoloration from nose and mouth
Smell of tobacco
Presence of leaves in stomach
Signs of respiratory obstruction

Medico-legal Importance

Accidental poisoning is common.
Tobacco is widely used as an addictive substance.
Not commonly associated with suicide or homicide.
