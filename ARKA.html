अर्क (ARKA)
Upaviṣa | स्थावर विष | Irritant Organic Vegetable Poison
Latin Name: Calotropis procera

English Name: Madar Tree
Local Name: Arka
Toxic Part: Latex, Root Bark
Modern Classification: Irritant Organic Vegetable Poison

Introduction

Calotropis procera (Arka), belonging to the family Asclepiadaceae, is an important medicinal plant widely found in India.

It is used in traditional medicine to treat diseases like asthma, cough, ulcers, skin disorders, and liver problems.

The plant shows many biological activities such as anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, wound healing, and anti-diabetic effects.

It also produces a milky latex used locally for treating fungal infections.

Habitat

Found commonly in dry, waste, roadside areas.

Grows easily throughout India, especially in warm regions.

Morphology

Plant Type: Shrub
Leaves: Opposite, thick, grey-green
Flowers: White with purple center, irritant
Fruit: Green inflated pods with silky seeds
Root: Hard, woody, branched
Colour: Yellowish brown
Taste: Bitter
Odour: Pungent
Poisonous Type: Latex

Active Principles

Calotropin
Calotoxin

Mode of Action (Viṣa Kriyā)

Acts as a local irritant and cardiotoxin.

Affects heart, liver, and nervous system.

Fatal Dose and Fatal Period

Fatal Dose:
Root bark – About 2–5 g
Latex – About 1–2 drops

Fatal Period: 6–12 hours after ingestion

Detoxification (Śodhana)

Latex purified with cow’s milk or gomutra.

Root bark boiled in Triphala decoction or milk.

Purification is mandatory before formulation use.

Management (Ayurveda)

Vamana Therapy:

Vamana is primarily indicated for elimination of vitiated Kapha Dosha. It induces vomiting to remove toxins from the stomach.

Indication: When poison is present in Amashaya.

Drugs used: Madana Phala Kashaya, Yashtimadhu Phanta.

Virechana Therapy:

Used for elimination of vitiated Pitta Dosha and expulsion of toxins.

Drugs used: Trivrut, Castor oil with Triphala, Danti and Dravanti.

Antidotes:
Neelini leaf juice, Chincha Patra Swarasa, Swarnagairika.

Poorvakarma:
Deepana, Pachana, Snehapana, Swedana.

Samsarjana Krama:
Gradual diet regimen to restore Agni.

Management (Modern)

Gastric lavage
Activated charcoal
Symptomatic treatment

Postmortem Appearance

Inflamed optic nerve
Froth from nose and mouth
Inflammation and ulcers in stomach and large intestine
Contraction of brain and meninges
Contraction of liver, spleen, and kidney

Mode of Use of Viṣa

Used externally after purification for treating pain, swelling, and skin disorders

Formulations

Mahavishgarbha Taila:
Provides relief from pain and inflammation, improves blood circulation, supports muscular health, reduces swelling, and relieves stiffness.

Dhanwantaram Taila:
Used in Vata disorders such as rheumatoid arthritis, osteoarthritis, spondylosis, headache, and neuromuscular conditions.

Treatment

Ayurvedic:
Administer Laksha Rasa, Ksheera or Kwatha orally. Apply Haridra leaf paste externally. Drinking water with Gairika neutralizes Arka poison.

Modern:
Wash stomach with warm water. Administer saline purgatives. Give demulcents and stimulants. Symptomatic treatment.

Medicolegal Aspects

Cattle poison
Arrow poison
Lacrimal abortification
Infanticide
