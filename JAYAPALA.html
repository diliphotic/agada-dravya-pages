जयपाल (JAYAPĀLA)
Upaviṣa | Drastic Purgative | Sthāvara Viṣa
Latin Name: Croton tiglium Linn.
Family: Euphorbiaceae
English Name: Croton Seed
Ayurvedic Classification: उपविष
Poisonous Part: Seeds (Croton oil)
Nature: Irritant Organic Vegetable Poison

Introduction

Jayapāla is a potent purgative drug used in Ayurveda and belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae. It is classified under Upaviṣa due to its strong toxicity and hence is administered only after proper Śodhana.

The seeds are mainly used for medicinal purposes. After purification, Jayapāla is indicated in severe constipation, ascites, abdominal disorders, and certain skin diseases.

Because of its drastic action, it should be given in very small doses and strictly under expert supervision.

Morphology

Habit: Medium-sized branching shrub or small tree; 4–10 meters tall
Stem: Erect, woody, branching; young stems green and smooth, older brown and rough
Leaves: Simple, alternate; ovate to elliptic; apex acute/acuminate; margin entire; smooth surface; long slender petiole
Inflorescence: Terminal or axillary racemose clusters
Flowers: Small, unisexual; monoecious; white to yellowish-green; male flowers above female flowers
Fruit: Capsule, small, globose, 1–1.5 cm diameter, characteristically three-lobed
Seeds: One seed in each lobe; oval, smooth, brownish; contain croton oil; highly toxic
Root: Tap root system; moderately developed; provides strong anchorage

Sources of Exposure

Traditional Medicine: Improper Śodhana, wrong dosage, self-medication, unqualified practitioners

Accidental Exposure: Children ingesting seeds mistaken for edible nuts; confusion with Eranda seeds; handling oil without awareness

Occupational Exposure: Herbal drug manufacturing, Ayurvedic pharmacies, inhalation of seed dust

Poisoning: Rare homicidal or suicidal use; occasional forensic cases in rural or tribal areas

Veterinary & Agricultural: Accidental ingestion by livestock through contaminated fodder

Cosmetic / External Use (Rare): Traditional use of oil for skin diseases

Mode of Action

Croton oil / Phorbol esters

↓

Stimulation of intestinal mucosa

↓

Irritation of intestinal lining with increased fluid secretion

↓

Increased peristaltic movement

↓

Rapid bowel movement

↓

Severe purgative action

↓

Vomiting and severe diarrhoea

↓

Loss of fluids and electrolytes

↓

Circulatory and respiratory collapse

Signs & Symptoms (Modern)

Hot burning sensation from mouth and throat up to abdomen
Excessive salivation
Nausea and vomiting
Gripping abdominal pain with bloody stools
Vertigo
Tachycardia
Circulatory collapse

Postmortem Appearance

Stomach & Intestine:
Intensely congested and inflamed mucosa, bright red to dark red coloration, swollen and oedematous, friable surface, patchy erosions, petechial or streaky hemorrhages, offensive fluid contents mixed with mucus and blood

Liver & Spleen:
Liver enlarged, congested, dark red to bluish-purple; cut surface oozes dark blood; engorged hepatic veins
Spleen enlarged, soft, congested; dark friable pulp with excess blood ooze

Kidneys:
Enlarged and swollen; capsule strips with difficulty; smooth congested surface; dark red color
