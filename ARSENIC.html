गिरीपाषाण (ARSENIC)
Poison of Metallic Origin
Name: White Arsenic / Vitreous Arsenic
Chemical Name: Arsenious Oxide
Chemical Formula: As₂O₃
Ayurvedic Name: गिरीपाषाण
Atomic Number: 33
Atomic Mass: 74.922

Introduction

Arsenic is a metallic poison commonly encountered as white arsenic (arsenious oxide). It is one of the most important homicidal and accidental poisons due to its easy availability and high toxicity.

Fatal Dose and Fatal Period

Fatal Dose: 180–200 mg
Fatal Period: 12–24 hours

Source of Exposure

Most common global exposure: Contaminated groundwater

Chronic exposure sources: Food (rice and cereals), air, soil

Acute exposure sources: Industrial accidents, pesticides, medicinal misuse

Signs and Symptoms

All capillaries dilated
Fall of blood pressure
Shock
Ventricular tachycardia
Death within 2–3 hours
Throat pain
Vomiting
Purging
Abdominal pain
Intense thirst
Diarrhoea with tenesmus
Burning sensation in GIT
Difficulty in swallowing
Headache
Vertigo
Tremors
Muscular cramps
Convulsions
Delirium
Coma and death
Hepatic: Fatty changes in liver, jaundice

Renal: Oliguria, anuria, albuminuria, renal tubular necrosis

Differential Diagnosis

Cholera
Food poisoning of bacterial origin

Treatment

Stomach wash
Emetics
BAL (Dimercaprol): 400–800 mg on 2nd and 3rd day every 4 hours
Glucose saline
Haemodialysis
Calcium disodium versenate
Penicillamine
Castor oil or magnesium sulphate to prevent intestinal absorption

Post-Mortem Appearance

Sunken eyeballs
Cyanosed skin
Signs of dehydration
GIT congestion with red velvety appearance of gastric mucosa
Lungs and brain congested and oedematous

Mode of Use of Arsenic in Viṣa Cases

After Śodhana: Purified using takra, triphala kvatha, godugdha, kanji or nimbu svarasa

Internal Use: Used in very minute doses mixed with ghṛta, madhu, or ārdrāraka svarasa

External Use: Lepa with haridra, chandana, and ghṛta

Formulations and Uses

रस माणिक्य: Chronic poisoning, Kāsa-Śvāsa, Kuṣṭha, Rājayakṣmā

रस सिन्दूर: Chronic toxic conditions, Jvara, Kuṣṭha

सोमाल योग: Dūṣī Viṣa, Gara Viṣa, Viṣaja Jvara

Tests to Detect Arsenic

Reinsch’s test
Marsh’s test
