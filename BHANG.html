भाङ्ग (BHĀṄGA)
Upaviṣa | Neurotoxic Poison | Madya Varga
Latin Name: Cannabis sativa Linn.
Family: Cannabinaceae
Sanskrit Name: भाङ्ग, मातुलानी
English Name: Indian Hemp
Category: Upaviṣa
Type of Poison: Neurotoxic Poison

Common Introduction

Bhanga (Cannabis sativa L.) is an ancient Upaviṣa medicinal herb valued for its potent therapeutic properties.

It is a narcotic plant, abundantly used during the Atharva Saṁhitā period.

It mainly acts on the central nervous system. Antidotes are rare, hence excessive usage should be avoided.

Introduction (Ayurvedic Perspective)

In Ayurveda, Bhāṅga is described as a semi-poisonous medicinal herb used after proper Śodhana and in precise dosage.

It is also known as Vijaya, Ganja, and Madinī and is considered potent when used judiciously.

Synonyms, Rasapañcaka & Classification

Synonyms: विजया, भाङ्ग, मातुलानी, गाञ्जा, मादिनी

रस: तिक्त
गुण: लघु
वीर्य: उष्ण
विपाक: कटु
वर्ग: मद्य वर्ग, औषध वर्ग

Active Principle

Resin – TetrahydroCannabinols (THC)

Fatal Dose & Fatal Period

Fatal Dose:
Bhanga – 10 g
Ganja – 8 g
Charas – 2 g

Fatal Period: 12–24 hours

Mode of Action

Bhāṅga primarily acts on the central nervous system causing stimulation followed by depression.

Signs & Symptoms (Modern)

Person remains cheerful
Enthusiastic and talkative
Increased appetite
Increased sexual desire
Sensation of smell like burning rope
Dizziness
Drowsiness
Dilated pupils
Deep sleep

Signs & Symptoms (Ayurveda)

सम्यक योग:
हर्ष, क्षुधावर्द्धक, अग्निदीपन, मेध्य, निद्राजनन

अतियोग:
मोह, भय, चिन्ता, बुद्धिनाश, नेत्रराग

Postmortem Appearance

External:
Body usually well nourished
Pupils normal or slightly dilated
Dry mouth and lips
Rare froth at mouth

Internal:
Stomach with greenish-brown semi-digested material and vegetable smell
Lungs mildly congested
Brain congested
Liver, kidney and spleen mildly congested without characteristic lesions

Śodhana

Swedana karma of Bhāṅga is done in Dolāyantra using godugdha for one hour, followed by Bhārjana karma with go-ghṛta.

Treatment

Modern Treatment:
Gastric lavage
Benzodiazepines
Antipsychotics like Haloperidol

Ayurvedic Treatment:
Pradhāna Karma:
Vamana – Madanaphala, Yaṣṭimadhu, Saindhava lavaṇa, Uṣṇa jala, Vacā
Virechana – Trivṛt, Aragvadha, Drākṣā
Dhūmapāna – Vacā, Haridrā, Devadāru, Guggulu
Nasya – Vacā svarasa, Anutaila, Śuṇṭhī svarasa, Go-ghṛta

Śamana:
Medhya & Viṣaghna lepa, Śīta jala śira snāna

Mode of Use in Viṣa Cases

Used as a viṣaghna dravya internally (pāna), nasya, lepa and dhūmapāna.

Used in Kapha-pradhāna Sarpa Viṣa conditions.

Management

Modern: Gut decontamination, benzodiazepines, haloperidol, supportive care

Ayurvedic: Nidāna parivarjana, Śodhana, Śamana, Pathya–Apathya

Detoxification Method

Dried leaves soaked in water, dried, then fried in ghee on manda agni.

Differential Diagnosis

मद्य
धतूरा
Hallucinogens
Epilepsy
Sedative poisoning

Morphology

Habit: Tall erect annual herb, ~3 m, dioecious
Stem: Erect, cylindrical, branched, rough, hairy
Leaves: Palmately compound, 5–9 serrated leaflets
Flowers: Small, greenish, unisexual
Fruit: Ovate achene with spiny growth
Flowering & fruiting: April–May

Formulations

Pushpadhanva Rasa – Vajīkaraṇa, Klaibya
Jatiphalādi Cūrṇa – Atīsāra, Grahaṇī, Chardi
Nidrodaya Rasa – Anidrā, Mānasika Vikāra
Bahumūtrāntaka Rasa – Bahumūtrata, Madhumeha
Mahā Jvarāṅkuśa Rasa – Jvara, Viṣama Jvara

Medicolegal Aspects

Mostly drug overindulgence or accidental poisoning
Rare homicidal use
Sudden poisoning common
