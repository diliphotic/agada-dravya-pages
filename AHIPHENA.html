अहिफेन (AHIPHENA / OPIUM)
Upaviṣa | स्थावर विष (Plant Origin)
Latin Name: Papaver somniferum
English Name: Opium, Poppy Plant
Family: Papaveraceae
Toxic Part: Latex
Ayurvedic Classification: उपविषद्रव्य
Modern Classification: Narcotic Poison

Introduction

अहिफेन is derived from the latex of Papaver somniferum (Opium poppy). It is classified as an Upaviṣa and used medicinally in small doses. Overdose causes toxic effects on the central nervous system.

Sources of Exposure

Dried opium collected from incised poppy capsules.
Cough syrups.
Oral ingestion, smoking, injection.

Botanical Information

कुल: अहिफेन
पर्याय: अहिफेन, अफुक, तीलभेद

Rasapañcaka of अहिफेन

रस: तिक्त, कषाय
गुण: सूक्ष्म, रुक्ष
वीर्य: उष्ण
विपाक: कटु
वर्ग: उपविषद्रव्य
Category: स्थावर विष (Plant origin)

Habitat

Cultivated in India, Turkey, Afghanistan, and Iran.

Major Indian Regions: Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh.

Morphology

Height: 3–4 feet
Leaves: Long, ovate, alternate
Flowers: Big, white, purple or red
Fruit: Capsule with milky latex (source of opium)
Seeds: Small, oval in shape
Taste: Bitter

Active Principles

Morphine (10–15%)
Codeine
Thebaine
Papaverine
Narcotine

Mode of Action (Viṣa Kriyā)

As per Ayurveda:
Acts as स्तम्भक
Suppresses body functions
Induces sleep

As per Modern:
Acts on µ-opioid receptors in the CNS
Causes analgesia, euphoria, and sedation
Depresses respiratory and cough centers

Fatal Dose and Fatal Period

Fatal Dose:
Opium: 2 g
Morphine: 200 mg
Codeine: 500 mg

Fatal Period: 6–12 hours

Signs & Symptoms (Ayurveda)

मूर्च्छा (Unconsciousness)
तंद्रा (Drowsiness)
निद्रा (Deep sleep)
श्वास (Respiratory distress)
मृत्यु due to प्राणवह स्रोतस अवरोध

Signs & Symptoms (Modern)

Restlessness
Confusion
Increased heart rate
Headache
Drowsiness
Heaviness in organs
Skin becomes cold
Respiration is reduced

Detoxification (Śodhana)

Soak latex in गोमूत्र or गोक्षीर
Boil with त्रिफला क्वाथ

Differential Diagnosis

Alcohol poisoning – odor of alcohol, no pinpoint pupils
Barbiturate poisoning – dilated pupils
Snakebite – presence of fang marks
Head injury

Management (Ayurveda)

वमन with मदनफल
विरेचन with त्रिवृत
Administer घृत with टंकन
स्नेह पान, स्वेदन, शीत जल

Management (Modern)

Gastric lavage with KMnO₄
Oxygen & ventilation
IV fluids
Perform other symptomatic therapy

Postmortem Appearance

Pinpoint pupils
Frothy discharge from nose & mouth
Congested lungs
Cold skin
Emaciation
Unabsorbed opium in stomach

Mode of Use in Viṣa Cases

Intentional (suicide)
Accidental overdose
Addiction
Criminal use

Medicolegal Aspects

Ideal suicidal poison (painless)
Rarely for homicidal use
Accidental poisoning in children (due to overdose)
