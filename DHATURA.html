DHATURA

Upavisha containing potent tropane alkaloids producing marked anticholinergic and cerebral effects.

Introduction

Dhatura is a tall erect annual or perennial herb of Solanaceae family. It is classified as an Upavisha in Ayurveda. All parts contain potent tropane alkaloids acting on the central nervous system.

Nomenclature

Sanskrit Name: Dhatura
Botanical Name: Datura metel Linn.
Family: Solanaceae
English Name: Thorn Apple
Synonyms: Matulaputraka, Kanaka, Unmatta, Matula
Varga: Upavisha

Source of Exposure

Accidental:
Mistaken seeds, improper decoctions, ingestion by children
Criminal:
Food and beverage adulteration, roadside stupefaction

Morphology (Macroscopic Characters)

Erect herb/shrub, 1–2 m height, unpleasant odour, stout cylindrical stem, green to purplish.
Leaves alternate, ovate, 10–20 cm; flowers large, white/violet, bisexual and actinomorphic.
Spiny capsule with numerous reniform brown-black seeds; seeds are highly poisonous.

Rasa Panchaka

Rasa: Tikta, Katu
Guna: Laghu, Ruksha
Virya: Ushna
Vipaka: Katu

Chemical Constituents

Atropine, Hyoscyamine, Hyoscine (Scopolamine), Dhaturin, Tropane alkaloids, Fixed oils

Toxicological Profile

Type of Poison: Cerebral poison
Fatal Dose: 50–100 seeds / 10–100 mg atropine
Fatal Period: ~24 hours

Mode of Action

Dhatura ingestion → Tropane alkaloids → Muscarinic receptor blockade → Parasympathetic inhibition → CNS stimulation → Delirium, mydriasis, dry skin

Signs & Symptoms

Dysphagia, drowsiness, dilated pupils, delirium, dry hot skin, ataxia, respiratory failure
Mukha Shosha, Jihva Parushata, Trishna, Unmada, Moha, Murchha, Jwara

Differential Diagnosis

Belladonna poisoning, Cannabis intoxication, Alcohol/drug intoxication, Mushroom poisoning

Post‑Mortem Appearance

Seeds in stomach, signs of asphyxia, congested organs, pulmonary edema, gastrointestinal inflammation, myocardial infarction in severe cases

Śodhana

Swedana Śodhana, Gomutra Śodhana, Patra Śodhana

Preparations & Uses

Bronchodilator, antispasmodic, antitussive; useful in Shwasa, Kasa.

Mahakanaka Taila: Analgesic, Vata‑shamana, neurological disorders.

Management

Modern:
Gastric lavage, Pilocarpine, supportive care (avoid morphine)
Ayurvedic:
Visha Chikitsa, milk with honey, Vrintaka phala rasa

Medicolegal Importance

Common roadside poison used in robbery, theft, kidnapping; accidental, suicidal and homicidal cases reported.

Research Notes

Antioxidant, antimicrobial, insecticidal and wound‑healing activities reported.
