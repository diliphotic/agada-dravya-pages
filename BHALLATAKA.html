भल्लातक (BHALLĀTAKA)
Upaviṣa | Irritant Organic Vegetable Poison
Latin Name: Semecarpus anacardium Linn.
Family: Anacardiaceae (Āmra Kula)
English Name: Marking Nut / Oriental Cashew
Ayurvedic Classification: उपविष
Modern Classification: Irritant Organic Vegetable Poison
Requirement: Mandatory Śodhana before use

Introduction

Bhallataka is one of the most powerful and fast-acting Ayurvedic drugs. It is extensively used in piles, skin diseases, and other chronic disorders.

Due to its अत्युष्ण and तीक्ष्ण nature, it is highly irritant and toxic in raw form and hence is used only after proper purification procedures.

Ayurvedic Identity

पर्याय (Synonyms): अग्निका, वीरवृक्ष, शोफकृत्, अग्निमुखी

रस: कटु, तिक्त, कषाय, मधुर
गुण: लघु, तीक्ष्ण, स्निग्ध
वीर्य: उष्ण
विपाक: मधुर
वर्ग: उपविषद्रव्य

Habitat & Morphology

Distribution: Tropical India – Western Ghats, Bengal, Central India.

Tree: Medium-sized deciduous tree
Leaves: Simple, leathery, oblong
Flowers: Greenish-white in panicles
Fruit: Smooth black drupe (marking nut)
Seed: Contains thick, oily, irritant juice

Chemical & Pharmacological Profile

Active Principles: Semecarpol, Anacardic acid, Bhilawanol, Cardol

Constituents: Bhallataka oil, flavonoids, phenolic compounds, triterpenoids

Pharmacological Actions:
Anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, antimicrobial, immunomodulatory, anti-arthritic, anti-cancer, metabolic enhancer, hepatoprotective, lipid-regulating

Mode of Action

Acts as a potent irritant causing intense local inflammation. Systemically it stimulates metabolism and immune response but produces severe toxicity if used without purification.

Toxicity & Fatal Data

Fatal Dose: 5–10 g
Fatal Period: 12–24 hours

Signs & Symptoms (Ayurveda)

कोष्ठताप – Burning sensation in abdomen
व्रण – Ulcer formation
स्फोट – Blister formation on skin (त्वचा)

Signs & Symptoms (Modern)

Local Application: Severe irritation, painful blisters, itching, bruise-like lesions

Ingestion: Throat blisters, severe GI irritation, dyspnoea, tachycardia

Detoxification (Śodhana)

Bhallataka nuts are tied in a cloth (पोटली) with brick powder and rubbed.

Brick powder absorbs the irritant oil.

Finally, nuts are washed with hot water to remove the outer skin, making them शुद्ध.

Management of Poisoning

Ayurvedic:
वमन, मृदु विरेचन
स्नेहन, स्वेदन
शीत लेप – चन्दन, उशीर, घृत
Antidotes: Cow ghee, milk, यष्टिमधु, शंख भस्म
Specific remedies: Milk, curd, coconut water; paste of castor leaves, black sesame, sandalwood

Modern:
Decontamination with soap and water
Gastric lavage with warm water and activated charcoal
Supportive care – IV fluids, analgesics, antihistamines, renal & hepatic monitoring

Medicolegal & Clinical Aspects

Differential Diagnosis: Contact dermatitis, gastritis, urethritis, stomatitis, allergic reactions

Post-mortem: Erosions of oral cavity and esophagus, liver & kidney congestion, blisters on exposed parts

Therapeutic Use (Post-Śodhana): Arśa, Kuṣṭha, Apasmāra, Gulma

Medicolegal Significance: Accidental poisoning, abortifacient use, punishment for infidelity, fabrication of injuries
