हृत्पत्री (DIGITALIS)
Cardiac Poison | Sthāvara Viṣa
Latin Name: Digitalis purpurea
Family: Scrophulariaceae
Sanskrit Names: हृत्पत्री, तिलपुष्पी, धूम्रपुष्पी, हृतपत्र
Type of Poison: Cardiac poison (हृदय प्रभावक)
Nature: Sthāvara Viṣa (Plant poison)
Active Parts: Leaves

Introduction

Digitalis is a well-known cardiotonic drug obtained from the plant Digitalis purpurea. It has been used in traditional as well as modern medicine for its powerful action on the heart, especially in cardiac failure and arrhythmias.

Due to its narrow therapeutic index, Digitalis is also classified as a potent cardiac poison.

Classification

Type of Poison: Cardiac poison (हृदय प्रभावक)
Nature: Sthāvara Viṣa
Family: Scrophulariaceae

Morphology / Identification

Root: Branched taproot system
Stem: Erect, unbranched, hairy, 1–2 m tall
Leaves: Basal rosette, alternate on stem, oval to lance-shaped, distinctly bitter
Flowers: Long bell-shaped, purple/pink/white/yellow with dark spots inside the tube
Fruit: Capsule containing numerous tiny seeds

Ayurvedic Properties (Rasapañcaka)

रस: तिक्त
गुण: लघु, रूक्ष
वीर्य: उष्ण
विपाक: कटु
दोष कर्म: कफ–वात शामक, पित्त वर्धक

Habitat & Distribution

Digitalis grows in disturbed or cleared lands, roadsides, burnt areas and rocky soils. It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade but tolerates full sunlight, cold climates and sea cliffs.

Mode of Action (Viṣa Kriyā)

Digitalis administration

↓

Inhibition of Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase pump in cardiac muscle

↓

↓

Traditional / Ayurvedic Perspective

According to Ayurveda, Digitalis possesses Tikta rasa, Laghu and Rūkṣa guna, Uṣṇa vīrya, Katu vipāka and Hṛdya prabhāva.

Toxicity occurs due to aggravation of Vāta and Pitta doṣa, primarily affecting the heart and gastrointestinal tract.

Modern Medical Perspective

In modern medicine, Digitalis glycosides are used in controlled doses for congestive heart failure and atrial fibrillation.

Toxicity occurs due to overdose or drug interactions, leading to life-threatening arrhythmias and gastrointestinal disturbances.

Fatal Dose & Fatal Period

Therapeutic Dose: 0.5–2 mg
Fatal Dose: 2–4 mg
Fatal Period: 0.5–24 hours

Detoxification / Śodhana

Gastric lavage (with caution due to arrhythmia risk)
Activated charcoal – 50 g orally, repeated every 4–6 hours
Ayurvedic Śodhana – Vamana and Virechana under strict supervision

Mode of Use in Digitalis Toxicity (Viṣa Cikitsā)

Viṣa-hara Cikitsā: Godugdha, Ghṛta, Madhu (with caution), Yaṣṭimadhu, Śatadhauta Ghṛta

Hṛdaya Rakṣaṇa: Arjuna, Drākṣā, Āmalakī

Therapeutic Uses

Congestive heart failure
Atrial fibrillation
Supraventricular tachycardia

Formulations & Indications

Digoxin tablets and injections – Cardiac failure
Ayurvedic formulations with Arjuna – Cardiac support

Postmortem Appearance

External: Cyanosis, vomit stains
Internal: Congested heart with subendocardial hemorrhages, dilated chambers; congestion of stomach, liver, kidneys, lungs and brain

Medico-legal Importance

Mostly accidental or suicidal poisoning
Rare homicidal use
Important in forensic toxicology due to cardiac effects

Identification Tests

Keller–Kiliani test
Baljet test
Legal test
