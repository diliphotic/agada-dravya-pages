कुपीलु (KUPĪLU)
Upaviṣa | Spinal Poison | Sthāvara Viṣa
Botanical Name: Strychnos nux-vomica
English Name: Poisonous Nut Tree
Family: Loganiaceae
Synonyms: कुचेलक, करस्कर, विषमुष्टि
Type of Poison: Spinal poison
Poisonous Part: Seeds (crushed)

Introduction

Kupīlu is a plant-origin Sthāvara Viṣa containing potent alkaloids such as strychnine and brucine, which primarily act on the central nervous system.

Due to its strong toxicity, it is classified as an Upaviṣa and is used therapeutically only after proper Śodhana.

Ayurvedic Properties (Rasapañcaka)

रस: कटु, तिक्त
गुण: रूक्ष, तीक्ष्ण
वीर्य: उष्ण
विपाक: कटु
वर्ग: उपविष

Availability

Commonly available in the form of herbal powders or cūrṇa prepared from raw seeds.

Toxicological Profile

Toxic Part: Crushed seeds
Seed Shape: Circular / discoid
Seed Size: 2–3 cm

Active Principles: Strychnine, Brucine, Loganine

Fatal Dose: 1–2 crushed seeds
Fatal Period: 1–2 hours

Morphology

Tree: Medium-sized evergreen tree
Habitat: Tropical regions of India
Leaves: Opposite, ovate with 2–3 veinlets
Flowers: Small, greenish-white
Fruit: Round, orange-yellow when ripe
Seeds: 2–3 cm diameter, disc-shaped, flat, greyish-green to brown

Sources of Exposure

Ingestion of raw Kupīlu seeds
Accidental intake through medicines without proper Śodhana
Intentional poisoning (homicidal or suicidal)
Adulteration and misuse in herbal formulations
Use as rodenticide

Mode of Action

Strychnine blocks inhibitory neurotransmitters (glycine) in the spinal cord, leading to uncontrolled reflex stimulation, severe muscle spasms, convulsions, respiratory failure, and death due to asphyxia.

Signs & Symptoms

Modern:
Nausea, anxiety, muscle twitching, dilated pupils, facial stiffness, cyanosis, severe convulsions

Ayurvedic:
मूर्च्छा, दाह, कम्प, स्तम्भ, तृष्णा, तोद / वेदना, आक्षेपण

Detoxification (Śodhana)

Go-mūtra Śodhana:
Remove outer shell of seeds, soak in cow’s urine for 7 days, wash, sun-dry, fry in ghee, powder and store.

Go-dugdha Śodhana:
Remove outer shell, tie seeds in cloth, boil in cow’s milk for 3 hours, wash with warm water, sun-dry, fry in ghee and store.

Differential Diagnosis

Feature Kupīlu Tetanus
Onset Sudden Gradual
Cause Poison ingestion Wound infection
Relaxation Present Absent
Consciousness Present Present
Duration Hours Days to weeks

Management

Modern:
Gastric lavage, activated charcoal, keep patient in quiet dark room, induce vomiting with warm water and salt

Ayurvedic:
Amāśaya prakṣālana (Yaṣṭimadhu phāṇṭa)
Raktamokṣaṇa (Jalaukā)
Vamana (Madanaphala)

Mode of Use

Cūrṇa, Avaleha, Vaṭi

Postmortem Appearance

Modern: Opisthotonus posture, cyanosis of lips and nails, congested edematous lungs, dark fluid blood, congestion of brain and viscera, undigested seeds in stomach, death due to asphyxia

Ayurvedic: आक्षेप, स्रोतसावरण, श्वासावरोध, रक्तदूष्टि, स्त्रोतोरुद्ध, अजीर्ण, प्राणक्षय

Medicolegal Aspects

Mostly accidental deaths
Used as cattle and arrow poison
Rarely used for suicidal purposes

Formulations & Indications

Kupīlu Vaṭi: Vātaja disorders
Viṣa Hindūja Vaṭi: Chronic fevers
Navjīvan Rasa: Nervous weakness
Agni Tuṇḍī Vaṭi: Indigestion
Lakṣmīvilāsa Rasa: Vāta vyādhi
