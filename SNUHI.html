स्नुही (SNUHI)
Irritant Poison | Upaviṣa | Sthāvara Viṣa
Latin Name: Euphorbia neriifolia Linn.
Family: Euphorbiaceae
Sanskrit Names: सुधा, स्नुक, वज्रद्रुम, वज्री
Vernacular Names: Milk hedge, Indian spurge tree, Male kalli
Poisonous Part: क्षीर (Milky Latex)
Modern Classification: Irritant Poison

Introduction

स्नुही is a drastic purgative plant found in rocky soil throughout India, usually growing as a small tree or large shrub. It is classified under Upavisha and belongs to the category of Sthavara Visha.

It is a succulent plant with milky latex and is considered semi-poisonous in Ayurveda. Traditionally, it has been used in the treatment of skin diseases, piles, and abdominal disorders.

Classification

  • Ayurveda: उपविष, क्षीरविष, स्थावरविष
  • Modern: Irritant poison

Habitat & Morphology

Habitat: Dry, rocky, and hilly regions.

  • Habit: Large erect glabrous shrub, up to 20 feet.
  • Leaves: Small, fleshy, rectangular; clustered at branch tips.
  • Stem: Cylindrical, green, fleshy with spiral ridges and sharp spines.
  • Flowers: Small yellow-green, arranged in cyme.
  • Fruits: Small, typically 3-lobed capsules.
  • Latex: Thick milky white latex exudes on cutting.
  • Seeds: Smooth, greenish-brown, mustard-sized.

Toxicological Profile

  • Poisonous Part: Milky latex
  • Active Principles: Euphorbine, Nerifoline, Taraxerol, Calcium mandelate, resins, gum, triterpenes
  • Fatal Dose: 125–250 mg
  • Fatal Period: 12–24 hours

Mode of Action (Viṣa Kriyā)

Snuhi visha acts as a strong irritant poison .It’s latex part contain diterpenoid esters that stimulate protein kinase C , leading to intense signs and symptoms.Its toxic actionis mainly due to its powerful irritant and inflammatory effects on tissues.

स्नुही क्षीर → सक्रिय घटक मुक्त (Phorbol esters, Triterpenoids, Proteolytic enzymes)

↓

Protein Kinase-C (PKC) Activation → Cell membrane irritation एवं damage

↓

Inflammatory Mediators का स्राव (Histamine ↑, Prostaglandins ↑, Cytokines ↑)

↓

स्थानीय ऊतक प्रभाव → दाह, फफोले (Vesication), कोएगुलेशन नेक्रोसिस

↓

Cauterizing एवं Debriding Effect

Source of Exposure (Viṣa Saṃsparśa Mārgāḥ)

  • Snuhi Kṣhīra:
    Contemporary science: Latex contains diterpenoid esters (phorbol esters)
    Routes of exposure:
    Skin contact → severe contact dermatitis, blistering
    Eye exposure → chemical conjunctivitis, corneal injury
    Oral ingestion → GI irritation, vomiting, diarrhea.
  • Tvak Sparśa:
    Irritant and vesicant action Leads to :
    eczema, erythema, ulceration, Occupational exposure in gardeners, plant handlers
  • Accidental Oral Ingestion:
    Ingestion of latex or plant parts causes:
    Gastrointestinal toxicity, Nausea, abdominal cramps, dehydration, More severe in children
  • Netra Saṃsparśa:
    Corrosive injury to eye can cause:
    Conjunctivitis, Keratitis

Signs & Symptoms (Lakṣaṇa)

External Contact:
1) Skin: Burning sensation
Vesication (blister formation)
Contact dermatitis.
Severe inflammation Ingestion can cause irritation and even death

2) Eyes: Excruciating pain and burning
Redness and irritation
Excessive lacrimation (tearing)
Lid edema (swelling) and conjunctival congestion
Blurred vision, which can potentially lead to temporary or permanent blindness

Internal Ingestion:
• Burning sensation in the throat and abdomen severe vomiting
• Diarrhea (which may be blood-mixed in severe cases) Abdominal discomfort/pain Convulsions
• Coma in severe cases of poisoning
• आध्मान, मूर्च्छा , प्रलाप, उदर दाह

Postmortem Appearance

1. Swelling and burning on the affected area.
Significant inflammation is observed in the mucous membranes where the poison made contact , such as the mouth, throat, stomach, and intestines.

2.Gangraneous patches on the stomach are seen.
It is the most notable finding in the presence of gangrenous (dead and decaying) patches in the severe corrosive action of the latex

3.Characteristic odour may bedetected in the stomach content.

4.Spleen condition: The spleen is often found in a ‘rotten’ condition , indicative of severe systemic effects.
